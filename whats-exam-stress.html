What is Exam Stress?

Exam stress is the tension and worry feelings you may experience when there's a test-taking situation. The stress that comes in general is a normal thing as it push individuals to work harder. It becomes serious when the stress interferes with your performance when taking the exam, resulting in you not showing your full potential.

"...Sometimes it's really hard to get myself to study. I procasinate a lot and have different methods to study really help to keep me engaged..."

-Anonymous Student

Signs of Exam Stress

Some signs of exam stress including (but not excluding):

Physical Signs

Shaking, Fast-heartbeat, upset stomach, trouble sleeping, etc.

Behavioural Signs

Fidgeting, nail-biting, increase/decrease in eating or drinking, etc.

Mental and Emotional Signs

Difficulties in concertation/focusing, feeling helpless, "going blank", worry , etc.

What causes it?

There's many reason why we have exam stress, but it is important to keep in mind that it is normal and majority experience it! Some causes maybe due to having lifestyles issue (such as not enough sleep, not eating properly etc.), not revising enough, and psychological factor (thinking you're not good enough or smart enough, or having a "all-or-nothing mindset"). There's many other causes and its important to know what cause it for you as it'll help on overcoming the stress!

How to overcome Exam Stress?

There's various steps you can take to overcome exam stress. There's 2 approach (skill/studying and behavioural/cognitive) you can take and 2 time-period (leading up to an exam and during exam time-period). It is your option on which approach and period to tackle the challenges you face but having combination of all of them works the best!

Leading up to an exam (1-2 months before the first scheduled exam)

- Ask for help in gaps you have

- Join a study group or make one

- Build a habit of revising materials you have learnt

When the exam starts

- Get organized the night before (Don't try to cram everything in one night be realistic!)

- Don't compare answers

- Try relaxation/mindfulness techniques

- Most importantly reward yourself!

Mentally

- Do Cognitive Reconstructing. Take notice when negative thoughts e.g. "I'm not smart enough" "What was I thinking when I choose this subject?". Those thoughts may amplify the stress, instead stop and try taking a different perspective.

- Talk to someone! If the stress keep building up it may have worse effects on you in the end. Talking someone about feeling stress about exams can help tremendously and take off weight on your shoulders.
